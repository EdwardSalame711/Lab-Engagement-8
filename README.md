# Site Narrative Final - Healthy Cultural Soul Food Options: Vegan vs. Carnivore Diets

## Promotes food awareness and community health, has a strong and educational purpose, and provides actionable steps (recipes, restaurant guides, and lifestyle comparisons).

## Phase 1 “Site Narrative”:

### Website Narrative 

### Title:   Soulful Bites
##### Soul Food Options: Balancing Tradition and Health

##### Description:
##### This website explores the evolution of traditional Soul Food within modern health trends. It promotes awareness of how cultural dishes can be reimagined to support healthier lifestyles—highlighting vegan and carnivore diet approaches, balanced nutrition, and community restaurants embracing cultural authenticity with wellness in mind.

##### Target Audience
##### Primarily African American and Latino adults aged 20–55 who value cultural heritage but seek healthier eating habits. Secondary audiences include nutritional students, community health advocates, and restaurant owners interested in culturally inspired healthy cuisine.


##### Purpose
##### To educate visitors about the historical and cultural importance of Soul Food while encouraging health-conscious alternatives that respect traditional roots. The site emphasizes how food choices can shape long-term wellness without abandoning identity or flavor.

##### Call-to-Action Statement
##### Latino and African American Community members should explore healthy Soul Food alternatives because tradition and wellness can thrive together and live Longer.



#### Sections

#### 1. Home – “Soul Food Reimagined”
##### •	Introduces the cultural roots of Soul Food and its deep connection to African American history.
##### •	Presents the site’s mission: preserving tradition while promoting nutrition and balance.
##### •	Includes a brief overview comparing vegan, vegetarian, and carnivore diets in a culturally relevant context.
#### 2. The Cultural Plate
##### •	Explores the origins of Soul Food staples such as collard greens, cornbread, fried chicken, and sweet potato pie.
##### •	Discusses historical influences (slavery, survival, and adaptation).
##### •	Provides insight into how these foods became symbols of community and resilience.
#### 3. Healthy Alternatives
##### •	Offers modern, health-forward takes on classic dishes—air-fried chicken, plant-based mac and cheese, and low-sodium greens.
##### •	Features comparative nutrition tables (Vegan vs Carnivore vs Balanced).
##### •	Highlights on how to modify recipes while maintaining flavor.
#### 4. Eat Local – Soulful Restaurants with a Twist
##### •	Directory of restaurants offering healthy or plant-based Soul Food options in key U.S. cities.
##### •	Each listing includes short profiles, chef stories, and menu highlights.
##### •	Option for visitors to submit their favorite healthy Soul Food spots.
#### 5. Learn & Share
##### •	Blog-style section featuring nutritionists and chefs discussing cultural eating habits.
##### •	User-generated stories on health transformations through cultural food awareness.
##### •	Links to external wellness resources and healthy cooking workshops.
